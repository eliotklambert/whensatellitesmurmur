Eliot Lambert / When Satellites Murmur

References

Annotated Bibliography


Dunne, Anthony, and Fiona Raby. The Secret Life of Electronic Objects. London, Bloomsbury, 2021.

This book delves into the invisible networks that shape our lives, especially those governed by electromagnetic forces. It introduced me to niche subcultures—whistler hunters, electrosensitives, and others whose experiences are deeply entangled with electronic technologies. I’m especially grateful to Dunne and Raby for their courses Tread Lightly and Who Comes After the Human (co-taught by Dominic Pettman), which inspired my research into starlings, satellites, and the more than human.


Dyer, Sophie, and Sasha Englemann. Open-Weather, https://open-weather.community Accessed May 8, 2025.

Discovering this international feminist community and archive was a turning point in my project, as I was able to use their open-source and accessible methodology to record satellite signals, enabling me to pick up real signals instead of using preexisting recordings.

Paik, Nam June. Good Morning, Mr. Orwell. 1984. U-Matic, color, stereo, 00:35:50. ZKM | Center for Art and Media.

This project, as well as the documentary ‘The Moon is the Oldest TV’ about Nam June Paik’s life, inspired me to recontextualize satellites and other technology for the purpose of art and poetry, transcending their functionality as tools of surveillance and military communication.

Teen-Age Message Concert. Performed by Lydia Kavina, Yana Aksenova, and Anton Kerchenko. Organised by Alexander Zaitsev, August 29 - September 4, 2001. Yevpatoria Planetary Radar, Yevpatoria, Crimea

Finding out about this concert felt serendipitous - that it was the first musical SETI message and they chose to communicate with a theremin is so fascinating to me and strengthened my belief that the theremin is the best instrument to intercept cosmic signals.

Tuma, Yuri.“Sound Ecologies and Ways of Listening”, Institute for Postnatural Studies (Zoom) November 5-December 17, 2024

This workshop series was very ear-opening in its exploration of interspecies deep listening, positing the question of whether we as humans are ready to listen to what other species have to say?

Sisters With Transistors: Electronic Music's Unsung Heroines. Rovner, Lisa,, et al. Widescreen and full screen versions. Kino Lorber, 2023.

This documentary introduced me to the women who pioneered electronic music (in the West). Witnessing the extent of their sonic experimentation at a time when electronic composition was not considered to be real music was very inspiring.
