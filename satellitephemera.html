Eliot Lambert / When Satellites Murmur

Process: Part Three - Satellite Ephemera


As most of my experimentation has been conducted on my roof, I have become increasingly intrigued by the satellite ephemera that surrounds me, particularly the circular dishes that receive radio waves and allow me to watch TV. Except I no longer need a satellite dish to stream Netflix, so the object has become a relic of a not-so-distant technological era - and in doing so, has evoked a sense of nostalgia in me. By projecting onto defunct circular satellite dishes, I intend to challenge sleek Starlink satellite dishes and their projection of accelerationism. Transforming the dish from a functional antenna into a screen, I am questioning what satellites are used for, and speculating what they could be used for. What if global connectivity was not about optimization and surveillance, but about actual, authentic connection and meaning-making? Like Zaitsgev’s ‘Teen Age Message’, Green’s ‘Gregnik’, or Paik’s ‘Good Morning Mr Orwell’, what if the satellite could transcend its functionality to become an art form?


I scoured Facebook marketplace for a defunct circular satellite dish, and ended up going upstate to pick one up, broken cables and all! The commute back to the city was tenuous and I’m glad I got my tetanus shot because I scratched my eyebrow with one of the rusty poles.
